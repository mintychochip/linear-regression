Brute-Force Linear Regression
on Army Training Data

A Log-Scale Grid-Search Approach to Ordinary Least Squares
Department of Computer Science & Engineering  |  California State University, Bakersfield  |  2026

Dataset Overview

The dataset consists of 50 U.S. Army trainees with two measured variables:

Predictor (X)
Height (inches)
Range: 64 – 79 in
Response (Y)
Weight (lbs)
Range: 135 – 275 lbs

Objective: estimate a linear model ŷ = β·x + α that minimizes the Sum of Squared Errors (SSE) across all observations.

Methodology: Log-Zoom Grid Search

  • Step 1 – Wide Search: Define a broad initial range for slope β and intercept α.
  • Step 2 – Log-Spaced Sampling: Sample candidate values on a logarithmic scale to maintain precision across orders of magnitude without clustering near zero.
  • Step 3 – Minimum SSE: Evaluate SSE for all grid pairs; select (β*, α*) with lowest error.
  • Step 4 – Zoom In: Shrink the search window by 10× around the best candidate and repeat.
  • Convergence: Process repeats until both β and α change by less than 0.0005 between iterations — guaranteeing 3 decimal places of stability. A hard cap of 50 iterations prevents infinite loops.

Conclusion

  • The log-zoom brute-force method successfully recovered the optimal OLS parameters without calculus or matrix operations.
  • Logarithmic spacing ensures no precision is lost when searching across wide parameter ranges.
  • Convergence is driven by a 3 decimal place stability criterion on both parameters — no fixed iteration count.
  • Applied to the test set (n = 10), the model produces height-based weight predictions consistent with training data trends.

Future work: extend to multiple regression and evaluate prediction interval coverage on held-out data.
